AUTHOR BIO:

My name is Michael. I’m an echocardiographer living in Austin who loves to research new things and lead a data-driven life. I have a wonderful family whom I love to spend my time with and I also enjoy some extracurriculars such as running, cycling, calisthenics and more. My background is deeply rooted in science, so naturally, I tend to approach new ideas or information with skepticism and adapt with it as it evolves.

Working in cardiology, I'm exposed to various types of people and diseases including the impact stress can have on the cardiovascular system over time. The symptoms typically range from palpitations and hypertension to inflammation and in the worst cases, a heart attack or stroke. Have you ever heard of "Heart-break disease?" The real name is Takotsubo and the etiology pertains to the presence of excessive stress. A rush of hormones actually inhibits part of the left ventricle from pumping and it requires many expensive tests and invasive procedures in order to diagnose. So I thought, if stress can have such a devastating effect on the body, wouldn’t the opposite also be true?

My career, love of learning and somewhat traumatic childhood inspired me to write Dare to be Happy. Although everything is great now, getting to this point was far from pretty and many hard lessons were learned along the way. I was no stranger to physical and emotional abuse (which I won't expand on too much here) and eventually, I ran away from my house at 16 years of age. This led to many years of couch-surfing, needing to move on a whim and barely skimming by with no real direction in life. Most of my furniture was sourced from dumpsters across the various apartment complexes I resided in and I had even gotten to a point where I could proudly move everything I owned within a matter of one hour. Somehow this unfortunate situation still found a way to stroke my ego. Regardless, living light was nice but the instability wasn't. There were times I was in-between places and I’ll never forget having to sleep in empty parking lots or having to stuff my trunk with non-perishable foods. Pro tip: you can purchase a gym membership to kill time, take showers and brush your teeth.

These traumas led to many self-deprecating behaviors that I’m certainly not proud of and although it’s difficult to admit, I was left unhappy all the way through my early 30s. Eventually, through a bit of luck and determination, I overcame my situation. It was ironically accomplished by leveraging my toxic behaviors such as my inflated ego or my toxic competitiveness and it also came with a price. For far too long, I had nobody in my life. We may have formed a temporary relationship but as soon as we got into any kind of trivial disagreement, I would quickly cut you out of my life and move on. That's just what I had learned growing up. I didn't see any value in quality relationships and I definitely didn't empathize or trust people. As a data-driven person, what I needed was to pair my situation and behaviors to an explanation. This is exactly what broke me free.

In 2022, I connected with a therapist. It was absolutely amazing because I was able to finally make these connections and gain greater control over my life. Turns out, my past was directly influencing my present and I had no clue! Without ever learning anything about emotional intelligence, reflection, effective communication etc, I was inclined to wearing my emotions on my sleeve and seeing them as absolute truths. These discoveries were revolutionary for me.

What kind of impact do you think newly discovered information like this would have on someone who loves to learn? You guessed it, more research and more learning. I quickly sought out to discover whatever I could about happiness. Every time I learned a new concept, I implemented it. After some practice, tweaking and reflection, I now had a framework that could be used by anybody and the entire process was fueled by my own happiness improving throughout the process. My relationships improved dramatically and I even managed to reconnect with "long lost" family whose relationships I had previously deemed as unsalvageable. I’m not necessarily a writer by trade but I just couldn’t keep this information to myself. It’s just too valuable.

So here I am, hoping to help you conquer your own personal struggles. I’ve blended science with experience and formulated a method that puts the control back in your hands. By the end of this book, I guarantee that your mindset will be different. Your relationships will fortify, your body will feel better and you’ll learn how to find peace through patience, empathy and self-compassion. I’ll begin with the science behind the “why,” then I'll end by helping you follow through with the “how.”
