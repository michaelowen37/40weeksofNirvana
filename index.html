Dare to be Happy




An unconventional, evidence-based guide that teaches you how to take charge of your life and prioritize your happiness.

To be read over the course of a year. 52 weeks in a year, 52 exercises in the book.



Each chapter, only a page in length, represents an important skill to be learned and developed over the course of a week. You'll practice, reflect, tweak then practice some more until these conscious level concepts turn into real, subconscious habits.

The information, sourced from psychiatrists, researchers, therapists and even monks, is conversational, relatable and easily-digestible. With each chapter we talk about the "why" then I follow up by helping you catalyze the "when."

This unconventional structure, I believe, will effectively teach you how to take charge of your own life.
It certainly had that effect on me.

AUTHOR BIO:

My name is Michael. I’m an Echocardiographer currently living in the Austin area who loves to research new things and lead a data-driven life. My background is in science, so naturally I tend to approach new information with skepticism and adapt with it as it changes. Working in cardiology, I see a wide variety of people and diseases including the impacts stress can have on the cardiovascular system when left unaddressed. The symptoms range from palpitations and hypertension to inflammation and in the worst cases, a heart attack or stroke. Interestingly, there’s another heart-related disease called Takotsubo which is brought on by excessive stress. People often refer to it as “heartbreak disease.” A rush of hormones actually causes part of the left ventricle to stop pumping and it requires many expensive tests and invasive procedures to diagnose. So I thought, if stress can have such a devastating effect on the body, wouldn’t the opposite also be true?

My career, love of learning and somewhat traumatic childhood inspired me to write Dare to be Happy. Although my life is great now, getting to this point was far from pretty and many hard lessons were learned along the way. I experienced a good amount of physical and emotional abuse and eventually ran away from my house at 16 years of age. That led to me sleeping on many couches and at times, homeless. I’ll never forget sleeping in the empty parking lots, stuffing my trunk with non-perishable foods and purchasing a gym membership just to kill time, take showers and brush my teeth.

These traumas led to many self-deprecating behaviors that I’m not proud of and although it’s difficult to admit, I was left unhappy all the way through my early 30s. Eventually, through a bit of luck and hard work, I was fortunate enough to overcome my situation. Ironically, I did this by leveraging some of my toxic behaviors such as my inflated ego or my toxic competitiveness but that also came with a price. For a long time, I had nobody in my life. I didn’t place any value in quality relationships and I was quick to cut people out over any trivial disagreement. At one point, I even thought that I could be a psychopath because my empathy and trust was simply nonexistent. I constantly struggled to pair my behaviors and solitude with an explanation. In fact, as I continued to sabotage my relationships, it wasn’t uncommon to hear me say “There’s something wrong with me.”

Eventually, in 2022, I connected with a therapist. It was amazing because I was able to finally make these connections with my childhood and gain greater control over my feelings. I discovered that my past was still directly influencing my present. I wasn’t ever taught how to effectively communicate, listen or handle my anger. I never even practiced self-reflection or emotional intelligence. All of this was revolutionary for me.

Being a data-driven person, I quickly sought out to learn as much as I could about happiness. I compiled information from trusted sources and actually tried to implement it. After some practice, tweaking and reflection, I now had a framework that could be used by anybody. My relationships improved dramatically and I even managed to reconnect with family I thought to be unsalvageable. I’m not necessarily a writer by trade but I just couldn’t keep this information to myself. It’s too valuable.

So here I am, hoping to help you conquer your own personal struggles. I’ve blended science with experience and formulated a method that inspires confidence and control. By the end of this book, your mindset will be different. Your relationships will fortify, your body will feel better and you’ll learn how to find peace through patience, empathy and self-care. Additionally, I’ll teach you how to step back and practice emotional intelligence and self-reflection. I’ll begin with the “why” and end with the “how.”
